Scrivix Tool Guide

A quick reference for Scrivix tools, gestures, overlays, and Mac pairing.

iPad Toolbar Options

Icon Tool & Description
📸 Screenshot Snapshot

Saves a clean screenshot of the current canvas or presentation area to your iPad Photos library. Toolbar controls are not included in the saved image.
🎨 Color Palette

Quickly switch between preset colors or choose a custom color. Long-press a preset color to save your current color for future sessions.
🖥️ Overlay & Whiteboard Menu

Tap the display icon to toggle the current overlay. Press and hold to choose whiteboard options including white, black, graph paper, lined paper, or a photo from your iPad.
〰️ Brush Tool

Draw smooth, pressure-sensitive ink with Apple Pencil. Brush size is remembered when switching between Brush and Highlighter.
🖍️ Highlighter

Adds translucent markup for emphasizing text, slides, diagrams, or documents without fully covering what is underneath.
💧 Fill Tool

Fill Smart Shapes or messy closed hand-drawn shapes with your selected color. Great for quick diagrams, emphasis boxes, and visual callouts.
🪄 Laser Pointer

Draw temporary laser marks that fade away automatically, perfect for pointing something out without leaving permanent ink behind.
🧽 Pixel Eraser

Erase part of a stroke by drawing over it, while leaving the rest of your annotation intact.
🧼 Stroke Eraser

Remove an entire stroke by swiping over it with your Apple Pencil or finger.
🗑️ Clear Screen

Clears your current annotations while keeping the active overlay or whiteboard background in place.
🖱️ Control Mode

Control your Mac from your iPad. Use Apple Pencil hover to move the Mac cursor, tap to click, double tap to double-click, tap and hold for right-click, tap-hold-drag to move windows or items, and two fingers to scroll.
✍️ Apple Pencil Only

Limit drawing input to Apple Pencil to help prevent accidental marks from your hand or finger while writing.
↩️ Undo

Step backward through recent annotation actions.
↪️ Redo

Restore a stroke or action that was removed with Undo.
🎚️ Brush Size Slider

Adjust the active tool size. Size options are disabled for tools that do not need them, such as Fill and Control Mode.
❌ Disconnect

Ends the session with your Mac and returns to the pairing screen.

Multi-Touch Gestures

Gesture Action & Description
✌️ Two-Finger Tap

Tap anywhere on the iPad screen with two fingers to instantly undo your last stroke without using the toolbar.
🤟 Three-Finger Tap

Tap anywhere on the iPad screen with three fingers to redo your last undone action.
↕️ Two-Finger Scroll

Scroll documents, webpages, slides, and other Mac content naturally. Two-finger scrolling also works while using drawing tools.

Smart Drawing

Feature Action & Description
▭ Smart Shapes

Draw rough rectangles, triangles, arrows, lines, or circles, then briefly hold your Apple Pencil down to snap the drawing into a cleaner shape.
⬛ Messy Shape Fill

The Fill Tool can also fill many closed hand-drawn shapes, even if they are not converted into Smart Shapes.

Connection Feedback

Status Meaning
🟢 Connected

Your iPad is connected to the Mac companion app and the live video stream is active.
🟠 Reconnecting

Scrivix is trying to restore the Mac connection or video feed. Keep both devices on the same local network.

Mac Application

Icon Tool & Description
🔐 Pairing Passcode

The Mac app displays a secure 4-digit code. Enter this code on your iPad to start a local Scrivix session.
✅ Permission Checklist

The Mac app shows whether Screen Recording and Computer Control permissions are enabled, with quick access to macOS Settings.
📡 Local Connection

Scrivix connects over your local network. Your drawings, screen stream, and controls are not sent to cloud servers.
🖥️ Mac Overlay

After pairing, Scrivix becomes a transparent full-screen overlay so you can annotate over presentations, documents, browsers, videos, and other Mac apps.
