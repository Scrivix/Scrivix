Scrivix Help & Support

Quick fixes for network connection issues and configuration snags.

📱 My iPad can't find or connect to my Mac. What do I do?

Because Scrivix beams data locally for ultra-low latency, both devices must be speaking to each other cleanly. Check these three things:
  • Same Wi-Fi Network: Verify that both your Mac and iPad are connected to the exact same Wi-Fi router network name (SSID).
  • Local Network Access: On your iPad, open the main iOS Settings app, scroll down to Scrivix, and make sure the Local Network toggle switch is turned ON.
  • AP Isolation: Public or corporate Wi-Fi networks (like coffee shops or offices) sometimes block local devices from seeing each other. If you are on a restrictive network, try using your iPhone's Personal Hotspot for both devices.

🛑 macOS is blocking the connection or drawing engine.

If your Mac app is running but nothing appears, your system firewall might be overprotective:
  • Go to Mac System Settings ➡️ Wallet & Privacy ➡️ Firewall.
  • If the Firewall is enabled, click Options... and ensure Scrivix.app is set to "Allow incoming connections".

⚡️ I am noticing lag or delayed stokes. How can I optimize it?

Scrivix targets a fluid, low-latency 120Hz update engine. If it stutters, it is almost always due to Wi-Fi congestion. Try moving closer to your Wi-Fi router, or connect your Mac to your router via Ethernet while keeping your iPad on the Wi-Fi loop.
